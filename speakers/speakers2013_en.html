TMP 2013 Invited Speakers

Vera Isaakovna Podlesskaya

Affiliation: RSUH (Russian State University for the Humanities)

Current Affiliation: Doctor of Philological Sciences, Professor at Institute of Linguistics, Russian Academy of Sciences

Research Interests: typology, Japanese linguistics, syntax of polypredicative constructions, oral discourse analysis, prosody.

Education and Career:

  • Graduated from the Department of Structural and Applied Linguistics, Moscow State University (1975)
  • Graduate studies at the Institute of Oriental Studies, Russian Academy of Sciences
  • PhD dissertation (1980): "Issues in Semantic-Syntactic Study of Modern Japanese Lexicon"
  • Doctoral dissertation (1994): "Complex Sentences and Other Types of Polypredicative Constructions in Modern Japanese. Theory and Typology"

Professional Activities:

  • 1975-1997: Worked at the Institute of Oriental Studies, USSR Academy of Sciences (Russian Academy of Sciences)
  • 1997-2022: Worked at RSUH, founder and head of the Educational-Scientific Laboratory of Linguistic Typology
  • Since 2023: Institute of Linguistics, Russian Academy of Sciences
  • Honored Professor of RSUH, Honorary Worker in the field of education of the Russian Federation
  • Author of more than 200 works on typology, Japanese linguistics, and syntax of polypredicative constructions

Ora Matushansky

Affiliation: CNRS/Université Paris VIII/UiL OTS/Utrecht University

Research Interests: Russian verb morphophonology (stress, hiatus resolution, ablaut), locatives, theoretical linguistics.

Ora Matushansky is an academic researcher at Utrecht University. Author of research in computer science and predicative expressions. Has an h-index of 14 and is co-author of 36 publications.

Professional Activities:

  • Previous affiliations: University of Paris & Centre national de la recherche scientifique
  • Currently works almost exclusively on Russian verb morphophonology
  • Specializes in stress, hiatus resolution, ablaut, and locatives
  • Active researcher in theoretical linguistics

Natalia Anatolievna Slioussar (Gensling)

Affiliation: Utrecht University/Saint Petersburg State University

Current Affiliation: HSE University (Moscow and Saint Petersburg)

Research Interests: theoretical and experimental linguistics, grammatical features and agreement, inflectional morphology in the mental lexicon, word order variation, information structure, syntax-prosody interface, architecture of grammar, generative approaches to Russian syntax.

Biography: Born in Saint Petersburg on November 16, 1980.

Research Areas:

  • Grammatical features and agreement (production, processing, and representation in mental grammar)
  • Inflectional and derivational morphology in the mental lexicon
  • Word order alternations (syntax and processing)
  • Information structure, especially formal approaches to it
  • Syntax-prosody interface
  • Architecture of grammar and psycholinguistically adequate grammars
  • Generative approaches to Russian syntax

Works in theoretical and experimental linguistics at HSE University in Moscow and Saint Petersburg.
