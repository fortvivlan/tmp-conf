TMP 2019 Invited Speakers

Maria Polinsky

Talk: Looking Right: Deriving VOS order in a V1 language

Affiliation: University of Maryland, Harvard University

Current Affiliation: Professor of Linguistics at the University of Maryland, College Park

Research Interests:

  • Syntactic theory
  • Language universals and the range of cross-linguistic variation
  • Heritage languages and their bearing on linguistic theory

Research Areas:

  • Various syntactic topics including long-distance dependencies, verb phrase structure, ergativity, and verb-initial orders
  • Primary work on a variety of languages: languages of the Caucasus, Austronesian, and Mayan languages
  • Structural properties of language spoken by unbalanced bilinguals (heritage languages)

Professional Activities:

  • Works with several research stations and labs
  • Expert in syntactic theory and typology
  • Specialist in heritage languages and bilingualism

Martin Haspelmath

Talk: Ergativity and depth of analysis

Affiliation: Max Planck Institute for the Science of Human History, Leipzig University

Current Affiliation: Department of Linguistic and Cultural Evolution, Max Planck Institute for Evolutionary Anthropology

Research Interests: comparative linguistics, studying the diversity of the world's grammatical and lexical systems, understanding what is universal about languages.

Major Achievements:

  • Co-editor of the "World Atlas of Language Structures" (2005/2013)
  • Co-founder of "Language Science Press"
  • Honorary (adjunct) professor at Leipzig University

Research Activities:

  • Comparative linguist studying the diversity of the world's grammatical and lexical systems
  • Researcher of language universals and what is universal about languages
  • Expert in typology and cross-linguistic comparison

Professional Activities:

  • Leading specialist in linguistic typology
  • Author of numerous works on grammar and lexicon
  • Active participant in the international linguistic community
