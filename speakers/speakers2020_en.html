TMP 2020 Invited Speakers

Mark C. Baker

Talk: On logophoric pronouns in African languages: Universals, variants, and larger comparisons

Affiliation: Rutgers University, USA

Current Affiliation: Professor of Linguistics at Rutgers University

Biography: Born in 1959. American linguist who received his Ph.D. from MIT in 1985.

Research Interests: generative grammar, polysynthetic languages, formal analysis of language structures.

Education and Career:

  • 1985: received Ph.D. from MIT
  • 1986–1998: faculty member at McGill University
  • Since 1998: teaching at Rutgers University
  • Frequent faculty member at the Linguistic Society of America's Summer Institute

Research Activities:

  • Worked with the Mohawk language for several years
  • Served as consultant on language revitalization for the Mohawk
  • Author of several books about the formal analysis of polysynthetic languages
  • Works within generative grammar framework

Marina Chumakina

Talk: Unusual agreement targets: Agreement or concord?

Affiliation: University of Surrey, UK

Current Affiliation: Research Fellow in Surrey Morphology Group (SMG)

Research Interests: typology, morphology, lexicography, syntax, language documentation and description, Nakh-Daghestanian languages.

Education and Career:

  • 2001: received PhD in Theoretical and Applied Linguistics from Lomonosov Moscow State University
  • 2001: joined Surrey Morphology Group
  • Research centre based in the School of Literature & Languages

Areas of Specialism:

  • Typology
  • Morphology
  • Lexicography
  • Syntax
  • Language Documentation and Description
  • Nakh-Daghestanian languages

Professional Activities:

  • Member of Association for Linguistic Typology
  • Member of Societas Linguistica Europaea
  • Member of Editorial Board, "Languages of the Caucasus"

Leonid Kulikov

Talk: Noun incorporation vs. nominal composition in a diachronic typological perspective: Evidence from Indo-European and beyond

Affiliation: Ghent University, Belgium

Current Affiliation: Research Fellow at Ghent University and Université catholique de Louvain

Research Interests: synchronic and diachronic linguistic typology, Vedic philology, Vedic verbal system and syntax, diachronic typology of voice, transitivity and valency-changing categories.

Education:

  • 1986: graduated from Moscow State University, Dept. of structural and applied linguistics
  • 1989: Candidate degree from the Institute of Oriental Studies, Moscow
  • 2001: PhD, Leiden University

Professional Career:

  • 1989-1993: Institute of Oriental Studies, Moscow
  • 1991-1993: Russian State University for the Humanities [RGGU], Moscow
  • 1993-2002, 2004-2011: Leiden University
  • 2002-2004: University of Nijmegen
  • 2005-2007: Göttingen University (Humboldt-fellowship)
  • 2009-2011: Institute of Linguistics, Moscow
  • 2011-2018, since 2021: Ghent University
  • Since 2021: Université catholique de Louvain

Editorial Activities:

  • Editor-in-Chief of the Journal of South Asian Languages and Linguistics (de Gruyter)
  • Member of editorial boards: Journal of Historical Linguistics, Moscow Linguistic Journal, Lingua Posnaniensis
  • Member of the Scientific Board of the series Mouton Handbook of Indo-European Typology
