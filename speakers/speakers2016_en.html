TMP 2016 Invited Speakers

Alena Witzlack-Makarevich

Affiliation: Kiel University

Current Affiliation: The Hebrew University of Jerusalem, Department of Linguistics, Faculty of Humanities

Research Interests: capturing, describing, and explaining linguistic diversity, grammatical relations, information structure, clause linkage, phonetics, conversation analysis, psycholinguistics.

Research Activities:

  • Functionalist linguistics track coordinator at The Hebrew University of Jerusalem
  • Combines large-scale typological studies involving several hundreds of languages with micro-typology and in-depth studies on individual languages
  • Actively involved in language documentation and description

Field Work:

  • Conducted multiple fieldtrips collecting primary data on Khoisan languages
  • Khoekhoe (Khoe-Kwadi) and Nǁng (or Nǀuu, ǃUi-Taa) spoken in South Africa
  • Currently working on the previously undescribed Bantu language Ruuli spoken in Uganda

Dmitry Vladimirovich Sichinava (Gensling)

Affiliation: (not listed in the programme)

Current Affiliation: Vinogradov Russian Language Institute RAS, HSE University

Research Interests: grammatical typology, verb, corpus linguistics, parallel corpora, pluperfect tenses in Slavic languages.

Education and Career:

  • Graduated from the Department of Theoretical and Applied Linguistics, Moscow State University (2002)
  • PhD dissertation (2005): "Typology of verbal systems with synonymy of basic paradigm elements" (supervisor V. A. Plungian)
  • 2002—2010: worked at VINITI RAS
  • Since 2011: Senior Research Fellow at the Vinogradov Russian Language Institute RAS

Professional Activities:

  • One of the initiators of the Russian National Corpus (participating since 2001)
  • Editorial board member of "Russian Language and Linguistic Theory" journal (since 2012)
  • Teaches at HSE University (since 2014): "History of Linguistics", "Diachronic Linguistics"
  • Author of "Typology of Pluperfect" (2013) and more than 100 publications

Iván Igartua and Nerea Madariaga

Affiliation: University of the Basque Country

Iván Igartua:

  • Born in Vitoria-Gasteiz in 1972
  • Degrees in Slavic Philology (Moscow Lomonosov University, 1995) and Hispanic Philology (Spanish Distance Learning University, 1997)
  • PhD in Classic Philology (University of the Basque Country, 2003)
  • Vice-Rector of the Álava Campus of the University of the Basque Country
  • Associate Professor of Slavic Philology (since 2009), accredited as Full Professor (since 2016)

Igartua's Research: historical linguistics, morphological typology, diachrony, historical morphology of Slavic languages, reconstruction of Proto-Basque, historical lexicography of the Basque language. Author of a dozen books and 76 papers in high impact publications.

Nerea Madariaga:

  • University of the Basque Country UPV/EHU
  • Specialization: Russian, formal syntax, diachrony
  • Co-author of research on Russian paucal constructions
