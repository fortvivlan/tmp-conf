2011 Conference

About the Conference

The inaugural conference "Typology of Morphosyntactic Parameters" took place in Moscow on December 5, 2011, at the Moscow State University for the Humanities named after M.A. Sholokhov (MSUH).

This historic conference marked the beginning of an annual series of conferences dedicated to typological research of morphosyntactic phenomena in the world's languages. The conference was organized with financial support from the Russian Foundation for the Humanities (project 11-04-00282a).

The first conference was organized in a workshop format, covering key areas of morphosyntactic typology: Russian and general grammar, polypredicativity and raising constructions, typology of relative constructions, problems of clitic typology in synchrony and diachrony, as well as various current problems of linguistic typology.

The conference brought together leading typology specialists from major Russian research centers and laid the foundation for the development of a regular scientific forum on morphosyntactic typology.

Historical Significance

The 2011 conference became the starting point for creating a permanent platform for scientific exchange in the field of morphosyntactic typological research. The success of the first conference contributed to:

  • Formation of a stable scientific community of morphosyntactic typology specialists
  • Establishment of the tradition of regular annual conferences
  • Development of an interdisciplinary approach to studying linguistic diversity
  • Creation of the foundation for future international collaborations

Over more than a decade of existence, the "Typology of Morphosyntactic Parameters" conference series has grown into a significant international scientific event, attracting researchers from around the world.
